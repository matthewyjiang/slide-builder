# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are presentation professionals who are comfortable working in a terminal. They use slide-builder while actively creating or refining a deck and want the interface to stay out of the way of that work.

Their core job is to create complete presentations and improve existing PowerPoint decks with equal confidence, without giving up native, editable PowerPoint output.

## Product Purpose

Slide-builder is a terminal-first, AI-assisted workspace for creating and refining native PowerPoint presentations. It brings conversation, agent activity, slide structure, live preview, and direct deck controls into one focused environment.

The product succeeds when users can achieve presentation-quality visual results with little design expertise.

## Positioning

The native PowerPoint builder designed for agentic terminal workflows.

## Brand Personality

Understated, technical, and focused. The interface should feel calm enough for sustained professional work, precise enough for a terminal-native audience, and purposeful rather than decorative.

Reference qualities:

- Rho's terminal-native density and conversation flow.
- Linear's disciplined hierarchy and restrained use of state color.
- Notion's calm, content-first workspace.

## Anti-references

Slide-builder should not resemble a noisy cyberpunk or hacker terminal, a generic chatbot with bubbles and repeated speaker labels, or a card-heavy SaaS dashboard. It should avoid a cute or overly playful assistant personality and prevent the conversation from becoming a dense wall of undifferentiated terminal text.

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear into the workflow.** Controls, status, and agent activity should remain legible without competing with the deck-building task.
2. **Make presentation quality attainable.** Help users reach strong visual outcomes without requiring deep design expertise.
3. **Treat creation and refinement as peers.** The experience should support starting from an outcome and precisely improving an existing deck with equal care.
4. **Use hierarchy before decoration.** Structure, spacing, typography, and restrained state color should carry meaning.
5. **Stay terminal-native.** Density, shortcuts, feedback, and interaction patterns should feel natural to professionals who choose a terminal workspace. Inherit the user's terminal palette rather than imposing a fixed visual environment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Maintain WCAG AA-equivalent contrast for text and essential interface elements.
- Support complete keyboard-only operation.
- Never communicate status or meaning through color alone.
- Remain usable in narrow terminals and terminals with limited color capability.
